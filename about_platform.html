About this site

Why I made this platform

I built this as a practical study tool for my students because I strongly believe in retrieval practice. One of the best ways to strengthen memory and identify knowledge gaps is to quiz yourself repeatedly, but many students are not sure how to do that in a way that is efficient, organized, and sustainable. My goal was to make that process simple.

What this platform is for

This site gives you quick access to practice questions I have created with the help of ChatGPT and then reviewed, edited, and organized for learning. The purpose is not to replace class, your textbook, or deeper studying. It is meant to help you actively test what you know so you can study more effectively.

Instead of passively rereading notes, you can sign in, choose your course, select the systems or content areas you want to practice, choose how many questions you want, and start quizzing right away. Each quiz gives you a random set of questions from the content areas you selected.

The main idea Keep practicing long enough and the system will naturally put more attention on the things you do not know yet. You do not have to micromanage that process yourself.

How the adaptive practice works

Every time you finish a quiz, the system keeps track of which questions you answered correctly and which ones you missed. Questions you get right become less likely to appear again soon. Questions you get wrong become more likely to reappear. Over time, that means your quizzes gradually adapt to your learning needs.

In other words, the platform tries to spend less time on content you already seem to know and more time on the content that still needs work. You do not have to sort questions manually or decide which specific topics to review next. The system handles that in the background while you keep practicing.

How to use it

1. Sign in Use your Google account to access the platform.
2. Choose your course Select the course that matches the content you want to study.
3. Pick your systems or topics Choose the content areas you want included in your quiz.
4. Choose the number of questions Do a short set for quick review or a longer set when you have more time.
5. Practice regularly The more consistently you use it, the more helpful the adaptive weighting becomes.

Why this is designed for quick practice

I wanted this to be easy to use in real life, not just when you are sitting down for a long formal study session. You can use it on your phone or on your computer, which makes it easier to fit retrieval practice into small pockets of time throughout the day.

On mobile You can quickly work through short quizzes whenever you have a few spare minutes.
On desktop You can move even faster by using the number keys 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 to choose answers on supported question types.
Best results Use it repeatedly over time rather than trying to do everything in one sitting.

Things to know

This is a learning tool It is designed to support practice, not to function as a graded exam platform.
Feedback helps improve the questions If a question seems confusing, unclear, or flawed, please use the feedback button so I can revise it more quickly.
Bugs are still possible This project required a lot of development and testing, but there is still a good chance that some of you will encounter glitches.
First troubleshooting step If something behaves strangely, try refreshing the page first. That solved several of the issues I ran into during testing.
Module codes are not active yet You may see a module code section, but there is nothing there for now. I hope to add pre-designed learning modules later.

How it works behind the scenes

The platform uses a script that pulls questions from Google Sheets and sends your quiz results back so the system can update what is more or less likely to appear next. Based on the documentation I reviewed, it should hold up even if many students are using it at the same time, but I have not fully stress-tested that yet.

Frequently asked questions

Are these questions perfect?

No. I have spent a lot of time testing and reviewing them, but this is still a live tool and some questions may need refinement. That is one reason the feedback feature exists.

Should I use this instead of other studying?

No. This works best as one part of your overall study plan. Retrieval practice is powerful, but it should complement your notes, readings, class activities, and deeper review.

What should I do if something looks wrong?

Refresh the page first. If the issue continues, let me know and include as much detail as you can about what happened so I can troubleshoot it.
